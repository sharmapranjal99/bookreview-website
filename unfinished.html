Unfinished

By Priyanka Chopra

Unfinished is Priyanka Chopra Jonas's memoir, offering an intimate look into her life as she navigated challenges and triumphs across continents. The book is a blend of her personal experiences, professional milestones, and reflections on her journey of self-discovery. Written with warmth and honesty, it highlights pivotal moments that shaped her into a global icon. The memoir begins with her early years in India, where she grew up in a military family with strong values. Her parents' careers as physicians in the Indian Army led to a nomadic childhood, exposing her to diverse cultures. Priyanka recounts her teenage years in the United States, which were marked by challenges such as racism and bullying. Despite these struggles, she developed resilience and a deep sense of ambition. Returning to India, she unexpectedly entered the world of beauty pageants, winning Miss India and subsequently, Miss World in 2000. These victories became a launchpad for her career in Bollywood, where she established herself as one of India's leading actresses. Priyanka candidly shares the obstacles she faced in a competitive industry, from battling gender biases to proving her versatility as an actor. The memoir also delves into her transition to Hollywood, where she faced the daunting task of carving out a space for herself as an Indian woman in a predominantly white industry. With determination, she broke stereotypes and achieved success with projects like Quantico. Priyanka also discusses her work as a producer, advocating for diverse stories through her production company. On a personal note, Unfinished reflects on her close bond with her late father, whom she describes as her greatest influence. His battle with cancer deeply affected her, and she shares how his memory continues to guide her. The book also includes glimpses into her relationship with her husband, Nick Jonas, portraying their love story as one of mutual respect and partnership. Beyond her career, Priyanka sheds light on her philanthropic efforts as a UNICEF Goodwill Ambassador and her commitment to education and empowerment initiatives. Her advocacy for girls' rights and equality is a recurring theme in the book. Unfinished is not just a recounting of Priyanka's accomplishments but also a philosophical exploration of identity, ambition, and self-belief. It encourages readers to embrace their individuality, take risks, and persevere despite setbacks. Through this memoir, Priyanka offers an inspiring narrative that resonates with anyone striving to overcome obstacles and achieve their dreams.
