Why Bharat Matters

--by S.Jaishankar

"Why Bharat Matters" is a thought-provoking and insightful book by S. Jaishankar, India's External Affairs Minister, that explores the significance of India's rise on the global stage. The book is a collection of essays that Jaishankar wrote during his tenure as India's Foreign Secretary, and it offers a unique perspective on India's foreign policy, its place in the world, and the challenges it faces in the 21st century.
Jaishankar's writing is clear, concise, and accessible, making the book an enjoyable read for both experts and non-experts alike. He draws on his vast experience as a diplomat and foreign policy expert to provide a nuanced and informed analysis of India's global engagement.
One of the key themes of the book is the idea that India's rise is not just a matter of national pride, but also a significant development for the world. Jaishankar argues that India's growth and influence can help to promote stability, prosperity, and democracy in the world.
Jaishankar also explores the challenges that India faces in its pursuit of great power status. He discusses the need for India to strengthen its economy, modernize its military, and enhance its global governance capabilities. He also highlights the importance of India's relationships with other major powers, including the United States, China, and Europe.
Throughout the book, Jaishankar emphasizes the importance of India's cultural and civilizational heritage in shaping its foreign policy and global engagement. He argues that India's unique blend of tradition and modernity can help to promote a more inclusive and diverse global order.
One of the strengths of the book is its ability to balance analysis and advocacy. Jaishankar is unapologetic about his enthusiasm for India's potential, but he also provides a clear-eyed assessment of the challenges that the country faces. He offers practical suggestions for how India can overcome these challenges and achieve its goals.
Overall, "Why Bharat Matters" is a must-read for anyone interested in India's foreign policy, its global engagement, and its rise as a major power. With its insightful analysis, clear writing, and nuanced perspective, the book is a valuable contribution to the ongoing debate about India's place in the world.
Rating: 5/5 stars
Recommendation: This book is perfect for anyone interested in India's foreign policy, its global engagement, and its rise as a major power. It is also a valuable resource for policymakers, scholars, and business leaders who want to understand India's role in the world and its potential for growth and influence.
