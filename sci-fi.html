SCI-FI

Homo Cosmicus 2: Titan: A Science Fiction Novel

Todor Bombov, 4.38 stars, 1,797 ratings, 13 reviews. The second book in the Sci-Fi series, set on Saturn’s moon Titan in 2069. The Earth’s civilization reaches a type-II status on the Kardashev scale, and Titan becomes humanity's golden mine for interplanetary trade. A mysterious series of deaths among the mission crew lead to a gripping sequence of events. A novel exploring the future of human civilization with GMO men, cyborgs, and androids.

Invisible Planets: Contemporary Chinese Science Fiction in Translation

Ken Liu (Editor, Translator), 4.03 stars, 5,049 ratings, 753 reviews. A collection of short speculative fiction from China, featuring both well-known and rising star authors. It includes award-winning stories and essays that explore the history and current state of Chinese science fiction.

Broken Stars: Contemporary Chinese Science Fiction in Translation

Ken Liu (Editor, Translator), 3.84 stars, 2,521 ratings, 409 reviews. A collection of sixteen short stories from China’s groundbreaking science fiction writers, edited and translated by award-winning author Ken Liu. The anthology includes stories about the future, technology, and society, along with essays on the rise of Chinese science fiction.

The Motion of Light in Water: Sex and Science Fiction Writing in the East Village

Samuel R. Delany, 4.32 stars, 1,144 ratings, 136 reviews. A memoir detailing Delany’s life as a black gay writer in 1960s New York, exploring his experiences in the bohemian Lower East Side and his development as a science fiction writer. Includes reflections on his relationships and interactions with other literary figures of the era.

So Long Been Dreaming: Postcolonial Science Fiction and Fantasy

Nalo Hopkinson (Editor), Uppinder Mehan (Editor), 4.00 stars, 814 ratings, 107 reviews. An anthology of original stories by African, Asian, South Asian, and Aboriginal authors, offering postcolonial perspectives on speculative fiction. The book includes new, bold, and imaginative stories addressing futures from the perspectives of marginalized writers.

Future Visions: Original Science Fiction Inspired by Microsoft

Jennifer Henshaw (Editor), Allison Linn (Editor), 3.61 stars, 1,145 ratings, 136 reviews. A collection of short stories inspired by the cutting-edge work at Microsoft Research. The authors explore the future of technology, including quantum computing, machine learning, and real-time translation, examining the relationship between these advancements and humanity.

The Future is Female! Women's Science Fiction Stories from the Pulp Era to the New Wave

Lisa Yaszek (Editor), Leslie F. Stone, Judith Merril, 4.10 stars, 659 ratings, 131 reviews. A groundbreaking anthology showcasing the work of visionary women writers in American science fiction from the 1920s to the 1960s, challenging the male-dominated narrative of the genre with stories about strange worlds, gender-bending aliens, and more.

Mortal Skies: A Science Fiction Horror Novel

Rebecca Fernfield, 4.22 stars, 58 ratings, 9 reviews. A gripping science fiction horror novel about a catastrophic event that causes chaos across the western hemisphere. As meteors crash and violence erupts, a father tries to protect his son from a brutal military quarantine and the dangers of a collapsing society.
