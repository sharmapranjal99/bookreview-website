The Queen Of Nothing

--by Holly Black

"The Queen of Nothing" is a captivating and enchanting conclusion to the Folk of the Air trilogy by Holly Black. The story picks up where the previous book left off, with Jude Duarte, the mortal queen of Faerie, navigating the complex and treacherous world of faerie politics.
Black's writing is as lush and evocative as ever, with a keen eye for detail and a deep understanding of the faerie world she has created. The characters are richly drawn and complex, with Jude being a particularly compelling and relatable protagonist.
One of the standout features of this book is its exploration of the complexities of power and morality. Jude is forced to confront the consequences of her actions as queen, and to navigate the gray areas between right and wrong. The book raises important questions about the nature of leadership and the responsibility that comes with power.
The world-building in the book is also noteworthy, with Black expanding on the faerie world she introduced in the previous books. The faerie courts are richly detailed and immersive, with a deep history and mythology that underpins the entire series.
The romance in the book is also well-handled, with Jude's relationships with Cardan and other characters being a key part of the story. The romance is sweet and tender, but also complex and nuanced, with a deep understanding of the characters' motivations and emotions.
Overall, "The Queen of Nothing" is a satisfying and enchanting conclusion to the Folk of the Air trilogy. With its lush world-building, complex characters, and nuanced exploration of power and morality, it is a must-read for fans of fantasy and faerie fiction.
Rating: 5/5 stars
Recommendation: This book is perfect for fans of fantasy and faerie fiction, particularly those who enjoy complex characters, nuanced world-building, and sweet romance. It is also a great choice for readers who enjoy books with strong female protagonists and explorations of power and morality.
