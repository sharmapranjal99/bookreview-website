The Village By The Sea

By Anita Desai

The Village by the Sea is a poignant story set in a small coastal village in India. The novel portrays the struggles and resilience of a family facing poverty, societal changes, and personal challenges. It is primarily centered on the lives of two siblings, Lila and Hari, as they navigate their harsh realities and seek a better future. The story begins in the village of Thul, where Lila, a thirteen-year-old girl, assumes the role of caretaker for her family. Her mother is gravely ill, bedridden, and unable to contribute, while her father is an alcoholic who squanders the little money they have on drink. Lila looks after her two younger sisters and manages the household despite the overwhelming hardships. Hari, her twelve-year-old brother, works tirelessly to support the family, often dreaming of escaping their plight. The family’s situation is exacerbated by the socio-economic changes sweeping through their village. A factory project threatens to disrupt the traditional way of life, bringing industrialization and environmental degradation. While the villagers are divided on whether the factory will bring prosperity or ruin, Hari sees it as a symbol of opportunity and decides to leave for Bombay (Mumbai) in search of work and a better future. In Bombay, Hari faces the harsh realities of urban life. He struggles to find work and food, ultimately securing a job in a small restaurant owned by a kind-hearted man named Jagu. Under the guidance of Mr. Panwallah, a compassionate watch repairer, Hari learns a trade, gaining skills and hope for a brighter future. His time in Bombay is transformative, instilling in him a sense of independence and determination. Meanwhile, back in Thul, Lila continues to manage the family’s affairs. She is supported by the De Silvas, a wealthy family from the city who vacation in the village. Their kindness provides temporary relief, but Lila remains burdened by her responsibilities. As the story unfolds, Hari returns to Thul, bringing with him the skills and confidence he acquired in Bombay. The siblings reconnect and begin to envision a future where they can overcome their struggles. Hari plans to start a small business repairing watches, while Lila remains the emotional anchor of the family. The novel concludes on a hopeful note, with Hari and Lila determined to carve out a better life for themselves and their family. While their journey is fraught with challenges, their resilience and resourcefulness symbolize the strength of the human spirit in the face of adversity. Desai’s narrative is rich with vivid descriptions of rural and urban India, exploring themes of poverty, family bonds, and the impact of modernization. Through the lens of Lila and Hari’s experiences, the novel highlights the struggles of marginalized communities while celebrating their enduring hope and perseverance.
