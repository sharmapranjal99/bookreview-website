SOCIAL-ISSUES

Unequal: How America's Courts Undermine Discrimination Law

By Sandra F Sperino, Suja A. Thomas. In this book, the authors reveal how American workers, particularly those alleging employment discrimination, face a biased legal system that systematically favors employers. Through procedural, evidentiary, and substantive mechanisms, discrimination cases are often dismissed before reaching trial. The book highlights the ongoing challenges employees face when seeking justice for racial or gender discrimination in the workplace. This authoritative account traces the history of employment discrimination and how it evolved into a system that makes it difficult for workers to access their legal rights.

Headlines: Halachic Debates of Current Events

By Dovid Lichtenstein. This book offers a halachic analysis of contemporary events, using Torah sources to address modern dilemmas. It explores how Orthodox Jews turn to halacha as a guide to navigating social and political changes in the world. The work provides readers with a fascinating perspective on Torah law and its relevance to current issues, offering both laymen and scholars valuable insights into the intersection of faith, law, and modern challenges.

General Knowledge Current Events & Who is Who 2014

By Unique Pub. This book compiles a variety of general knowledge information related to key personnel and major events in the year 2014. It offers a quick reference for those interested in staying up-to-date with important figures and events from that year, making it a useful resource for trivia and general knowledge enthusiasts.

Virginity or Death!: And Other Social and Political Issues of Our Time

By Katha Pollitt. A compelling collection that addresses the inconsistencies and hypocrisies in modern social and political issues. With sharp humor and moral clarity, Pollitt critiques the ways in which societal structures and political ideologies hinder progress. Her essays challenge readers to reconsider the issues surrounding sex education, abortion, and fundamentalism, offering a witty yet profound commentary on the state of contemporary society.

Social Structure, Issues and Policies

By Telugu Akademi. This book examines the concept of social structure, which refers to the patterned social arrangements in society. It explores various levels of social organization, from macro structures like class stratification to micro-scale interactions within social networks. The work delves into how these structures shape human behavior and social policies, providing a comprehensive view of the dynamics that influence societal issues.

Sex Trafficking: The Global Market in Women and Children

By Kathryn Farr. This book offers a detailed exploration of sex trafficking, documenting the global scale of the industry and its devastating impact on women and children. Farr examines the economic, social, and criminal dynamics that fuel trafficking, shedding light on the systemic problems that perpetuate it. The book provides a sobering look at the human cost of sex trafficking and calls for a more robust response to combat this global crisis.

Government Ruins Nearly Everything: Reclaiming Social Issues from Uncivil Servants

By Laura Carno. This book critiques the role of government in solving personal and societal problems, arguing that government intervention often leads to failure rather than success. Carno explores various public issues, including healthcare, education, and marriage, offering a case for reclaiming these issues from governmental control. The book serves as a call to action for citizens to take back power from ineffective bureaucracies.

The Future of Higher Education

By Dan Clawson, Max Page. This book explores the crisis in higher education, focusing on issues like rising tuition, the decline of public funding, and the assault on tenure. Clawson and Page analyze the current state of higher education in the U.S. and propose a plan for revitalizing the system. Their work offers insights into the politics of higher education and addresses the need for reform in a rapidly changing educational landscape.
